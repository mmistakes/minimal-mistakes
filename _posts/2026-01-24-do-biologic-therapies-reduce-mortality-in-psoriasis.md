<h2>What Are Biologic Therapies?</h2>
<p>Biologic <b>therapies are targeted</b> systemic treatments designed to block specific immune pathways involved in psoriasis inflammation.</p>
<h3>Common Biologic Targets</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>TNF‑α inhibitors</strong></li>
<li><strong>IL‑17 inhibitors</strong></li>
<li><strong>IL‑23 inhibitors</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>Unlike traditional systemic medications, biologics directly reduce the inflammatory signals responsible for both <strong>skin disease and internal inflammation</strong>.</p>
<p>👉 <em>Related cluster:</em><br />
<strong>Systemic <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2025/07/infection-vs-inflammation-guide.html" rel="dofollow" title="Infection vs. Inflammation: Know Your Body's Defenses"><b>Inflammation in Psoriasis</b></a>: Beyond the Skin</strong></p>
<hr />
<h2>Why Inflammation Drives Mortality in Psoriasis</h2>
<p>Chronic inflammation contributes to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Atherosclerosis</li>
<li>Endothelial dysfunction</li>
<li>Insulin resistance</li>
<li>Plaque instability in blood vessels</li>
</ul>
<p>These processes explain why <strong>cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death</strong> in <b>people with psoriasis</b>.</p>
<p>👉 <em>Related cluster:</em><br />
<strong><a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2026/01/psoriasis-and-cardiovascular-disease.html">Psoriasis and Cardiovascular Disease: What Patients Need to Know</a></strong></p>
<hr />
<h2>Evidence Linking Biologic Therapy to Reduced Mortality</h2>
<h3>Cardiovascular Outcomes</h3>
<p>Multiple observational studies suggest that patients treated with biologics experience:</p>
<ul>
<li>Reduced vascular inflammation</li>
<li>Improved endothelial function</li>
<li>Lower rates of major adverse cardiovascular events compared to untreated or topically treated patients</li>
</ul>
<p>TNF‑α inhibitors have shown the <strong>strongest association</strong> with cardiovascular risk reduction in long‑term data.</p>
<hr />
<h3>All‑Cause Mortality</h3>
<p>Emerging evidence indicates:</p>
<ul>
<li>Lower overall mortality in biologic‑treated patients compared to those receiving no systemic therapy</li>
<li>Reduced inflammation‑related complications</li>
<li>Improved long‑term disease control</li>
</ul>
<p>While randomized mortality trials are limited, real‑world registry data consistently support a <strong>protective trend</strong>.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Do All Biologics Offer the Same Benefit?</h2>
<p>Not necessarily.</p>
<h3>Current Evidence Suggests:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>TNF‑α inhibitors:</strong> Most consistent cardiovascular benefit</li>
<li><strong>IL‑17 inhibitors:</strong> Strong inflammation control; long‑term mortality data still evolving</li>
<li><strong>IL‑23 inhibitors:</strong> Promising safety and metabolic profiles; mortality data ongoing</li>
</ul>
<p>Ongoing research continues to refine risk‑benefit comparisons.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Biologics and Metabolic Risk</h2>
<p>By reducing systemic inflammation, biologics may:</p>
<ul>
<li>Improve insulin sensitivity</li>
<li>Lower inflammatory lipid abnormalities</li>
<li>Reduce <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2026/01/the-cellular-pillar-autophagy-metabolic.html" rel="dofollow" title="The Cellular Pillar: Autophagy &amp; Metabolic Signaling"><b>progression of metabolic</b></a> syndrome</li>
</ul>
<p>This is particularly important for patients with overlapping cardiometabolic risk.</p>
<p>👉 <em>Related cluster:</em><br />
<strong><a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/04/psoriasis-metabolic-syndrome-mortality-risk.html">Metabolic Syndrome Criteria and Health Risks in Psoriasis</a></strong></p>
<hr />
<h2>Important Limitations to Understand</h2>
<p>While evidence is encouraging:</p>
<ul>
<li>Most mortality data are observational</li>
<li>Patients on biologics may receive better overall care</li>
<li>Individual <b>cardiovascular risk factors still</b> matter</li>
</ul>
<p>Biologic therapy should be part of a <strong>comprehensive risk‑reduction strategy</strong>, not a standalone solution.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Who May Benefit Most From Biologics?</h2>
<p>Patients with:</p>
<ul>
<li>Moderate to severe psoriasis</li>
<li>Psoriatic arthritis</li>
<li>Coexisting metabolic syndrome</li>
<li>High cardiovascular risk profiles</li>
</ul>
<p>Early intervention may offer the greatest long‑term benefit.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Clinical Takeaways</h2>
<ul>
<li>Psoriasis‑related inflammation increases mortality risk</li>
<li>Biologic therapies reduce systemic inflammation</li>
<li>Evidence suggests lower cardiovascular and all‑cause mortality</li>
<li>TNF‑α inhibitors show the strongest long‑term data</li>
<li>Integrated care remains essential</li>
</ul>
