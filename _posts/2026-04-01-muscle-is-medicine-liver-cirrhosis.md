For nearly thirty years, seniors diagnosed with advanced liver disease (cirrhosis) were given a terrifying set of instructions: **"Restrict your protein."** The logic was to "rest the liver." 

In **April 2026**, we know that advice was not only outdated—it was dangerous.

## The Problem: Starving the Body to Save the Liver
When you limit protein, your body starts "internal cannibalism." It breaks down your own muscles to get the nitrogen it needs for the immune system and brain. This leads to **Sarcopenia** (extreme muscle loss).

In cirrhosis, muscle loss is the single strongest predictor of mortality. Without muscle, seniors cannot recover from infections or maintain metabolic balance.

## The 2026 Standard: Muscle as Filtration
Your muscles act as a secondary "filtration system" that helps process toxins like ammonia. The more muscle you have, the less work your liver has to do. To protect that muscle, 2026 protein targets have doubled.

### The Protein Target Shift (80kg Patient)

| Metric | Legacy Approach (Pre-2026) | 2026 Advocacy Standard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Philosophy** | Restrict protein to lower stress. | Maximize protein to fight frailty. |
| **Calculation** | 0.8g per kg of body weight. | **1.2 to 1.5g per kg of weight.** |
| **Daily Goal** | 64 grams of protein. | **96 to 120 grams of protein.** |

## "Protecting the Pipes": The Blood Pressure Link
If you have **Varices** (swollen veins in the esophagus), keeping your blood pressure below **130/80 mmHg** is non-negotiable. Think of it like turning down the tap on a garden hose to prevent weak spots from bursting.

## Actionable Steps: The Douglas Method
1.  **Challenge the Old Advice:** Ask your hepatologist: *"Is it safe for me to increase to 1.2–1.5g of protein to protect my strength?"*
2.  **The Bedtime Snack:** Eat a small, protein-rich snack right before bed. This prevents the liver from "eating" your muscle for fuel overnight.
3.  **Monitor Your BP:** Keep your bottom number (diastolic) in the 70s to protect delicate esophageal veins.

### FAQ: Metabolism & Muscle in Cirrhosis

**Q: What is "metabolic starvation"?**
**A:** It's when the liver can't release energy normally, so the body burns muscle tissue for fuel. This speeds up frailty even if you think you are eating "enough."

**Q: Why does ammonia cause muscle wasting?**
**A:** When the liver fails, muscle tissue tries to detoxify ammonia. This process "burns through" amino acids and destroys muscle fiber.

**Q: Do BCAAs (Amino Acids) help?**
**A:** Yes. Branched-chain amino acids support muscle synthesis and can reduce episodes of hepatic encephalopathy (brain fog).
