## Why Heart Disease Is a Major Concern in Psoriasis

Moderate-to-severe psoriasis significantly increases an individual's statistical risk of developing **cardiovascular disease (CVD)**, making heart attacks, strokes, and heart failure the leading causes of mortality among patients managing this condition. Extensive clinical registry data shows that this elevated risk persists even after adjusting for traditional risk factors like smoking, body mass index, and age. This points directly to **chronic systemic inflammation** as the primary driver of vascular degradation. 

When your immune system is persistently hyper-activated, it shifts your blood vessels into a state of chronic vulnerability, accelerating tissue damage far beneath the surface of the skin.

## How Psoriasis Attacks Your Blood Vessels

The chronic immune-mediated overdrive that characterizes psoriasis causes continuous damage to the **endothelium**—the delicate, single-cell inner lining responsible for regulating blood vessel relaxation and contraction. 

[Image of endothelial cell dysfunction showing normal blood vessel vs inflamed vessel with plaque accumulation]

This persistent cytokine storm leads to three distinct cardiovascular failure points:

### 1. Accelerated Atherosclerosis
In patients with psoriasis, the accumulation of cholesterol plaques along the arterial walls happens much earlier and with significantly greater aggression compared to the general population. Chronic low-grade inflammation acts like a chemical match, constantly burning the vessel wall and forcing fats to calcify rapidly.

### 2. Elevated Arterial Stiffness
As circulating immune complexes continuously irritate the vascular matrix, smooth muscle tissues lose their natural elasticity. This narrowing increases resting systemic blood pressure, forces the heart to pump against higher resistance, and reduces overall cardiac output efficiency.

### 3. Plaque Instability (The "Soft Plaque" Trap)
The most dangerous mechanism involves how inflammatory cytokines, specifically **TNF-α and IL-17**, interact with existing cholesterol deposits. These cytokines actively dissolve the protective fibrous cap that seals away arterial plaque, rendering it "soft," thin, and highly unstable. When blood pressure spikes, these fragile caps can rupture, exposing the plaque to the bloodstream and instantly triggering a major heart attack or ischemic stroke.

## The Cross-Talk: The Psoriatic-Metabolic Syndrome Overlap

The severe vascular friction caused by psoriasis does not operate in a vacuum. The identical cytokine surges that irritate your blood vessels simultaneously block insulin receptors on your muscle and liver tissues, worsening peripheral insulin resistance. 

In clinical geroscience, this persistent cellular insulin resistance inside the brain is recognized as a primary driver of neuro-degeneration—a concept researchers call [**Type 3 Diabetes**](/posts/brain-health-is-alzheimers-really-type-3-diabetes/).

## 🎯 May 2026 Clinical Update: Vascular Resilience Targets

- **Vascular Integrity Target:** Maintain your resting systemic blood pressure strictly under 130/80 mmHg to significantly reduce the pounding, turbulent physical force of blood against inflamed, fragile vessel linings.

- **Advanced Protein Sourcing:** To maintain continuous skin cell turnover while actively protecting lean muscle mass during systemic therapy, older adults must target 1.2 to 1.6 grams of high-quality protein per kilogram of body weight daily.

- **Targeted Plaque Compaction:** Landmark multi-center clinical trials demonstrate that utilizing advanced biologic therapies (specifically targeting TNF-α and IL-17 pathways) does more than clear skin plaques—it actively reduces the structural volume of dangerous, non-calcified "soft" coronary plaque over a 12-month timeline.

## 📖 Plain-Language Glossary of Terms

- **Atherosclerosis:** The progressive narrowing and hardening of the arteries caused by a buildup of cholesterol plaques, accelerated by chronic immune system activation.

- **Biologics:** Laboratory-engineered protein therapies designed to precisely target and neutralize specific inflammatory cytokines in the bloodstream.

- **Endothelium:** The thin, delicate layer of cells lining the interior surface of blood vessels, responsible for regulating blood pressure and structural cardiovascular safety.

- **hs-CRP (High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein):** An ultra-precise blood biomarker that measures systemic low-grade inflammation, functioning as a primary tool to evaluate future cardiac risks.

- **Plaque Rupture:** The tearing or breaking of an unstable arterial plaque's fibrous cap, which causes sudden blood clotting and can instantly trigger a heart attack or stroke.
