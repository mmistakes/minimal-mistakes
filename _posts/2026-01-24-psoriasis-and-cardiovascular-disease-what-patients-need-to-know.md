<h2>How Psoriasis Affects the Heart and Blood Vessels</h2>
<p>Psoriasis triggers persistent immune activation that extends far beyond the skin.</p>
<h3>Key Cardiovascular Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Chronic endothelial inflammation</li>
<li>Accelerated atherosclerosis</li>
<li>Increased arterial stiffness</li>
<li>Plaque instability</li>
</ul>
<p>Inflammatory cytokines such as <strong>TNF‑α, IL‑17, and IL‑6</strong> <b>damage blood vessels and promote plaque formation</b>, increasing the likelihood of heart attacks and strokes.</p>
<p>👉 <em>Related cluster:</em><br />
<strong><a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2026/01/systemic-inflammation-in-psoriasis.html">Systemic <b>Inflammation in Psoriasis</b>: Beyond the Skin</a></strong></p>
<hr />
<h2>Cardiovascular Conditions Linked to Psoriasis</h2>
<h3>1. Coronary Artery Disease</h3>
<p>People with psoriasis develop <b>coronary artery disease</b> <strong>earlier</strong> and <strong>more aggressively</strong> than the general population.</p>
<ul>
<li>Higher plaque burden</li>
<li>Increased myocardial infarction risk</li>
<li>Greater likelihood of silent heart disease</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h3>2. Stroke</h3>
<p>Chronic inflammation increases clot formation and vascular damage, leading to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Higher ischemic stroke risk</li>
<li>Increased stroke severity</li>
<li>Worse long‑term outcomes</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h3>3. Hypertension</h3>
<p>Psoriasis is strongly associated with:</p>
<ul>
<li>Treatment‑resistant high blood pressure</li>
<li>Endothelial dysfunction</li>
<li>Increased cardiac workload</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h3>4. Heart Failure</h3>
<p>Long‑standing inflammation contributes to:</p>
<ul>
<li>Myocardial remodeling</li>
<li>Reduced cardiac efficiency</li>
<li>Increased heart failure risk over time</li>
</ul>
<hr />Chronic heart strain and inflammation can also lead to <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2023/09/anemia-what-you-need-to-know.html">Anemia of Chronic Disease</a>, creating a cycle of fatigue.
<h2>Does Psoriasis Severity Matter?</h2>
<p>Yes — cardiovascular risk <b>increases with psoriasis</b> severity.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Severe psoriasis:</strong> Highest <b>cardiovascular and mortality</b> risk</li>
<li><strong>Moderate psoriasis:</strong> Significantly elevated risk</li>
<li><strong>Mild psoriasis:</strong> Still higher risk than the general population</li>
</ul>
<p>Importantly, <strong>cardiovascular inflammation may progress even when skin symptoms improve</strong>.</p>
<hr />
<h2>The Role of Metabolic Syndrome</h2>
The same insulin resistance that complicates psoriasis is now being linked to <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2026/02/alzheimers-type-3-diabetes-insulin-resistance.html">Cognitive Decline (Type 3 Diabetes)</a>
<p>Metabolic <b>syndrome amplifies cardiovascular risk in psoriasis</b>.</p>
<p>When combined, psoriasis and metabolic syndrome create:</p>
<ul>
<li>Severe insulin resistance</li>
<li>Accelerated vascular damage</li>
<li>Markedly increased mortality risk</li>
</ul>

<h2>Can Treating Psoriasis Reduce Cardiovascular Risk?</h2>
<h3>Systemic and Biologic Therapies</h3>
<p>Evidence suggests that targeted psoriasis treatments may:</p>
<ul>
<li>Reduce vascular inflammation</li>
<li>Improve endothelial function</li>
<li>Lower inflammatory biomarkers linked to heart disease</li>
</ul>
<p>Biologic therapies targeting <strong>TNF‑α and IL‑17</strong> show particular promise.</p><p>Recent studies, including the <i data-index-in-node="49" data-path-to-node="8,0">VASCULAR</i> trials, suggest that biologic therapies like TNF-inhibitors (Humira) and IL-17 inhibitors can actually <b data-index-in-node="161" data-path-to-node="8,0">reduce the volume of 'soft' coronary plaque</b>—the dangerous kind that leads to heart attacks. By controlling my psoriasis flare-ups with biologics, I am also helping protect my heart from a second event.</p>
<hr />
<h3>Lifestyle and Preventive Care</h3>
<p>Cardiovascular risk reduction requires a comprehensive approach:</p>
<ul>
<li>Blood pressure control</li>
<li>Lipid management</li>
<li>Weight reduction</li>
<li>Smoking cessation</li>
<li>Regular physical activity</li>
</ul>
<p>Psoriasis patients benefit most from <strong>integrated dermatology and primary care management</strong>.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Screening Recommendations for Psoriasis Patients</h2>
<p>Routine cardiovascular screening is essential, especially for patients with moderate to severe disease.</p>
<h3>Recommended Monitoring</h3>
<ul>
<li>Blood pressure</li>
<li>Lipid profile</li>
<li>Blood glucose or HbA1c</li>
<li>BMI and waist circumference</li>
<li>Family history assessment</li>
</ul>
<p>Early detection significantly reduces preventable cardiovascular events.</p>

<li>Chronic systemic inflammation is the primary driver</li>

</ul>

  <h2 id="faq-psoriasis-cardiovascular-heading">
    Psoriasis and Cardiovascular Risk – FAQ
  </h2>

  <div class="faq-item" id="psoriasis-heart-disease">
    <h3 class="faq-question">
      Does psoriasis increase heart disease risk?
    </h3>
    <p class="faq-answer">
      Yes. Psoriasis increases the risk of heart attack, stroke, hypertension,
      and heart failure due to chronic vascular inflammation.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="faq-item">
    <h3 class="faq-question">
      Is cardiovascular risk higher in severe psoriasis?
    </h3>
    <p class="faq-answer">
      Yes. Cardiovascular risk increases with psoriasis severity, but even mild
      disease carries higher risk than the general population.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="faq-item">
    <h3 class="faq-question">
      Should psoriasis patients be screened for heart disease?
    </h3>
    <p class="faq-answer">
      Routine cardiovascular screening is recommended, especially for patients
      with moderate to severe psoriasis or additional risk factors.
    </p>
  </div>
