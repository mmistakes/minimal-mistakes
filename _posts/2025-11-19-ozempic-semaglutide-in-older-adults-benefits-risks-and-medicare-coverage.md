  <h2 style="text-align: left;">Ozempic in Older Adults: Clinical Benefits, Muscle Risks, and the 2026 Medicare Landscape</h2>

  <p>For seniors, Ozempic® (semaglutide) has become a revolutionary tool for managing Type 2 Diabetes and reducing the risk of heart attack and stroke. But as an independent researcher and a survivor of a major heart attack myself, I know that this "miracle" drug requires a tactical approach. For us, the goal isn't just weight loss—it is <strong>Vascular Resilience</strong> and <strong>Muscle Preservation</strong>.</p>

  <div class="ah-card">
    <h2 class="ah-title">The "Three-Way" Mechanism: How it Protects You</h2>
    <p>Semaglutide is a <strong>GLP-1 receptor agonist</strong>. It mimics a hormone your body naturally releases after eating, performing three vital tasks:</p>
    
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Glucose Regulation:</strong> Stimulates insulin only when your blood sugar is high.</li>
      <li><strong>Appetite Suppression:</strong> Tells your brain you are full, reducing the "food noise."</li>
      <li><strong>Gastric Slowing:</strong> Keeps food in the stomach longer, stabilizing post-meal sugar spikes.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="ah-update-box">
    <h3 class="ah-title" style="color: #1d4ed8;">🔬 March 2026 Clinical Synthesis: Sarcopenia &amp; BP</h3>
    <p>As of <strong>March 2026</strong>, we are seeing a critical shift in how Ozempic is prescribed for seniors:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>The 1.2–1.5 g/kg Protein Rule:</strong> To prevent sarcopenia (muscle wasting), seniors on GLP-1s must hit a high-protein target of <strong>1.2–1.5 grams per kilogram</strong> of body weight. Without this, you may lose muscle mass, leading to falls and frailty.</li>
      <li><strong>The 130/80 BP Target:</strong> For heart attack survivors like me, the cardiovascular benefits of Ozempic are best realized when systemic blood pressure is maintained at <strong>&lt;130/80 mmHg</strong>.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
