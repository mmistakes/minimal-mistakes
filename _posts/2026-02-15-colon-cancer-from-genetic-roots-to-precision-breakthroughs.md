  <div class="ah-card">
    <h2 class="ah-title">The 2026 Swedish Breakthrough: The MakA Toxin</h2>
    <p>Researchers at <strong>Umeå University</strong> have <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/08/crispr-potential-healthcare.html" title="Crispr Potential Healthcare" rel="dofollow"><b>discovered a potential</b></a> "smart bomb" for colorectal cancer (CRC) utilizing a toxin called <strong>MakA</strong>, produced by the cholera bacteria.</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Targeted Lethality:</strong> Unlike chemotherapy, MakA <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/03/healthy-after-65.html" title="Healthy After 65" rel="dofollow"><b>ignores healthy cells and specifically accumulates inside</b></a> tumor cells.</li>
      <li><strong>The Mechanism:</strong> Once inside, it triggers a "self-destruct" sequence (apoptosis) and <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/07/mind-over-matter-brain-immune-system.html" title="Mind Over Matter Brain Immune System" rel="dofollow"><b>alerts the immune system to attack</b></a>.</li>
      <li><strong>Safety Profile:</strong> Early 2026 data shows effective tumor shrinkage without the systemic side effects typical of traditional treatments.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="ah-card">
    <h2 class="ah-title">🧬 Precision Prevention: The ALASCCA Trial</h2>
    <p>The ALASCCA Trial from the <strong>Karolinska Institutet</strong> is a discovery families can discuss with doctors today.</p>
    <div class="ah-accent-card">
      <strong>The Key Discovery:</strong> Patients with a mutation in the <strong>PIK3 signaling pathway</strong> (approx. 40% of patients) see a massive reduction in recurrence when taking daily low-dose aspirin. Biomarker testing for this mutation is now critical.
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="ah-card">
    <h2 class="ah-title">🧬 Family-History Screening Path</h2>
    <p>Since my daughter is over 40 and had a first-degree relative pass young, her path follows the <strong>10-Year Rule</strong>.</p>
    <div class="ah-accent-card">
      <strong>The 10-Year Rule:</strong> Screening should begin at <strong>age 40</strong>, or <strong>10 years earlier</strong> than the youngest diagnosis in the family—whichever comes first.
    </div>
    <h3>What to Ask Your Specialist:</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>"Is there evidence of <strong>Lynch Syndrome</strong>? (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2 mutations)"</li>
      <li>"Can we perform a <strong>PIK3 Mutation test</strong> to see if low-dose aspirin is a viable adjunct?"</li>
      <li>"Is <strong>ctDNA (Liquid Biopsy)</strong> appropriate for monitoring between colonoscopies?"</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
