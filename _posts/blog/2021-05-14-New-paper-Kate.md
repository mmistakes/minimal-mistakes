Congrats to Kate, Esther, and Tom with their new paper that just got accepted in Psychonomic Bulletin & Review! :fireworks:
   
In this work they investigated the role of agency in reward prediction error (RPE)-based learning. Earlier research showed that signed RPEs (SRPEs; "better-than-expected" signals) drive declarative learning. However, it is unknown whether this RPE must derive from the participantâ€™s own response, or whether instead, any RPE is sufficient to obtain the learning effect. To test this, RPEs were generated in the same experimental paradigm where an agency and a non-agency condition were combined. In the current paper, no interaction was observed between RPE and agency, suggesting that any RPE (irrespective of its source) can drive declarative learning.

Reference:
- Ergo, K., De Vilder, L., De Loof, E., & Verguts, T. (2021). Reward prediction errors drive declarative learning irrespective of agency. Psychonomic Bulletin & Review, 1-12.
