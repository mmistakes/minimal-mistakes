<h4 style="font-size: 1.2rem;"><strong>Phase 1: Your Yard (The "7 Ts" Strategy)</strong></h4>
<div style="background-color: #fdfdfd; border-top: 3px solid rgb(0, 77, 64); color: #444444; margin-top: 20px;">  
<ul style="font-size: 1.1rem;">
  <li>
    <p>[ ] <strong>Tip:</strong> Flip over plant saucers, toys, and pet bowls daily. <i>Aedes</i> mosquitoes can breed in a bottle cap of water.</p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p>[ ] <strong>Toss:</strong> Recycle old tires or containers that hold stagnant water.</p>
  </li>
  <li>
    <p>[ ] <strong>Turn:</strong> Wheelbarrows and plastic pools should be stored upside down.</p>
  </li>
  <li>

<h3>2026 Mosquito-Borne Disease FAQ</h3>
<p><strong>Q: Can I catch Dengue or Zika from a "winter" mosquito in the South?</strong>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>A:</strong> In 2026, the term "mosquito season" is becoming obsolete in the Deep South. While activity slows below <strong>50°F</strong>, urban heat islands and mild winters in cities like New Orleans, Houston, and Miami allow <i>Aedes</i> mosquitoes to survive year-round in storm drains and crawl spaces. If you have a fever and <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/january-thyroid-awareness-month/" rel="dofollow" title="Empower Your Butterfly: January is Thyroid Awareness Month  | AGING HEALTH"><b>rash in January</b></a>, do not rule out a mosquito-borne cause.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Why should I avoid Ibuprofen if I suspect a mosquito bite fever?</strong>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>A:</strong> This is a critical safety point. Many mosquito-borne illnesses, particularly <strong>Dengue</strong>, can <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/why-oxalates-could-cause-major-health/" rel="dofollow" title="Oxalates - Why They May Cause Health Issues | AGING HEALTH"><b>cause your platelet</b></a> count to drop. Ibuprofen (Advil/Motrin) and Aspirin are blood thinners that can increase the risk of internal bleeding. Until a <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/ent-doctor/" rel="dofollow" title="Ent Doctor"><b>doctor confirms your diagnosis</b></a>, stick exclusively to <strong>Acetaminophen (Tylenol)</strong> for <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/science-of-pain-causes-management/" rel="dofollow" title="Science Of Pain Causes Management"><b>pain and fever</b></a>.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Does "Oil of Lemon Eucalyptus" <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/what-is-crispr-how-does-it-work/" rel="dofollow" title="What Is Crispr How Does It Work"><b>actually work as well</b></a> as DEET?</strong>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>A:</strong> Yes, but with a catch. CDC-approved <strong>OLE (or PMD)</strong> is the only plant-based repellent that rivals DEET in efficacy. However, OLE typically requires more frequent reapplication-roughly every <strong>2-3 hours</strong> in high Southern humidity-whereas 25% <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/the-best-technology-advances-of-last/" rel="dofollow" title="A New Age: Major Medical Advances in the Last 5 Years | AGING HEALTH"><b>DEET can last</b></a> up to 8 hours.</p>
<p><strong>Q: If I've had Chikungunya once, can I get it again?</strong> <strong>A:</strong> Current research suggests that a single <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/infection-vs-inflammation-guide/" rel="dofollow" title="Infection Vs Inflammation Guide"><b>infection with Chikungunya</b></a> likely provides <strong>lifelong immunity</strong>. However, you can still contract Dengue (which has four different strains) or Zika, as the antibodies do not cross-protect against different viruses.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Is "Microcephaly" still the only concern with Zika in 2026?</strong>&nbsp;</p><p><strong>A:</strong> While pregnancy remains the highest risk, 2026 data show a stronger correlation between Zika and <strong>Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)</strong> in adults-a rare <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/mind-over-matter-brain-immune-system/" rel="dofollow" title="Mind Over Matter: Brain's Control Over the Immune System | AGING HEALTH"><b>condition where the immune system attacks</b></a> the nerves. If you experience tingling or weakness in your legs following a mosquito-related illness, <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/posts/medical-debt/" rel="dofollow" title="Medical Debt"><b>seek medical attention immediately</b></a>.</p><div style="background-color: #fdfdfd; border-top: 3px solid rgb(0, 77, 64); color: #444444; font-size: 1.1rem; margin-top: 20px;"><button onclick="document.body.classList.toggle('audit-mode')">Toggle Sources
