<p>&nbsp;</p><h2><strong>Sources &amp; Citations Policy</strong></h2><p>At Aging Health, accuracy and trust are at the core of everything we publish.
This page explains how we select, evaluate, and cite the medical and scientific sources used throughout our articles.</p><h3><strong>1. Commitment to Evidence‑Based Information</strong></h3><p>All health content on Aging Health is grounded in reputable, authoritative sources.
We rely on:</p><ul><li><p>National Institutes of Health (NIH)</p></li><li><p>Mayo Clinic</p></li><li><p>Cleveland Clinic</p></li><li><p>Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)</p></li><li><p>World Health Organization (WHO)</p></li><li><p>PubMed and peer‑reviewed medical research</p></li><li><p>Government health agencies</p></li><li><p>University medical centers</p></li></ul><p>We do <strong>not</strong> use anecdotal claims, unverified remedies, or speculative medical theories.</p><h3><strong>2. How We Select Sources</strong></h3><p>We prioritize sources that are:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Peer‑reviewed</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Clinically validated</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Published by recognized medical institutions</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Updated regularly</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Transparent about methodology</strong></p></li></ul><p>We avoid sources that lack scientific backing or promote unproven treatments.</p><h3><strong>3. How We Cite Sources</strong></h3><p>Each article includes:</p><ul><li><p>Inline citations</p></li><li><p>A list of key sources</p></li><li><p>Links to original research when available</p></li><li><p>Clear attribution to medical organizations</p></li></ul><p>We cite sources to allow readers to verify information and explore topics further.</p><h3><strong>4. Updates to Sources</strong></h3><p>Medical knowledge evolves.
When new research becomes available, we:</p><ul><li><p>Update articles</p></li><li><p>Replace outdated citations</p></li><li><p>Add new evidence</p></li><li><p>Note significant revisions when appropriate</p></li></ul><h3><strong>5. Transparency &amp; Integrity</strong></h3><p>We do not accept payment for including medical sources or linking to external sites.
All citations are chosen solely for accuracy and educational value.</p><h3><strong>6. Reader Trust</strong></h3><p>Our goal is to empower older adults, caregivers, and families with reliable, easy‑to‑understand health information.
Clear sourcing is a key part of that mission.</p>
