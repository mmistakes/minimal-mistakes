    <p>With age, physiological changes—such as reduced arterial elasticity and heart muscle thickening—can elevate the risk of heart failure and <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/01/watchman-device-prevent-clots.html" target="_blank">arrhythmias</a>. One primary driver is <strong><a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2026/01/apob-analysis-heart-health-longevity.html" target="_blank">atherosclerosis</a></strong>, where fatty deposits (plaque) accumulate in arterial walls, narrowing blood flow to critical organs and increasing the risk of heart attacks and strokes.</p>

    <h3>The Hidden Burden of Valve Disease</h3>
    <p>Heart valves that control blood flow can become stiff (stenosis) or weak (regurgitation) over time. Recent data indicates that over <strong>one in four seniors</strong> are affected by <strong>asymptomatic valve disease</strong>—conditions that show no outward symptoms but pose a significant risk of heart failure. This prevalence jumps from 21% in those aged 60-64 to over 53% in those 85 and older.</p><p><br /></p><table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><tbody><tr><td style="text-align: center;"><img alt="Side‑by‑side medical illustration comparing a healthy aortic valve with wide, flexible leaflets to a stenotic valve with thickened, calcified, narrowed leaflets. This reduced flow can lead to chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting, or heart failure if untreated.</td></tr></tbody></table><br /><p></p><h2>Statins for the Elderly: A Personal and Clinical Perspective</h2>
    <p>Statins are the standard for lowering cholesterol and preventing plaque buildup. However, their use in the "very old" remains a point of clinical discussion. <strong>Personally, I cannot tolerate statins</strong> due to the severe leg and muscle cramps they cause, a side effect that, while manageable for some, can significantly impact quality of life for others.</p>
    
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Benefits:</strong> Proven reduction in heart attack and stroke risk, even in those without prior history.</li>
      <li><strong>Risks:</strong> Potential for muscle pain (myalgia), fatigue, and interactions with other medications (polypharmacy).</li>
      <li><strong>The Balance:</strong> Statin therapy should be a tailored conversation between the patient and provider, weighing longevity benefits against day-to-day functional health.</li>
    </ul>

    <p>Current treatments effectively manage the <em>symptoms</em> of heart disease, but the frontier of cardiology is shifting toward addressing the <em>mechanisms</em> of aging itself.</p>
