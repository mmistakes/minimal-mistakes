How to Use the Aging-Health Research Library
Welcome to Aging-Health. This site is designed to help you navigate the complex world of clinical research without the medical jargon. Here is how to get the most out of our 2026 research library:

1. The Research Sidebar (Your "Cheat Sheet")
On the right-hand side of every article, you will see a "Research Highlights" box.

What it is: A summary of the most critical data points in the post.

How to use it: If you are in a hurry, look here for the "Target Values" or "Key Targets" (like protein grams or GFR numbers).

2. Clinical Grids and Calculators
Many of our posts include interactive tools, such as our Blood Pressure Category Calculator or Daily Glucose Tracker.

Calculators: Enter your numbers to see where you fall within the 2026 clinical guidelines.

Trackers: Use the provided links to open your own private copy of our health trackers in Google Docs or Excel.

3. Navigating by Category
If you are looking for information on a specific part of your health, use the top navigation menu:

brain-mind: Focuses on cognitive resilience and neurological health.

heart-metabolic: Covers vascular defense, blood sugar, and heart health.

Longevity: Explores the latest 2026 science on aging gracefully and maintaining muscle.

4. Join the Conversation
At the bottom of every post, you will find a Giscus Comment Box.

Ask Questions: If a clinical term is confusing, ask for a "Health Literacy" translation.

Share Experience: Connect with other caregivers and seniors navigating similar health journeys.

Note: You will need a GitHub account to post, which helps us keep our community free of medical spam.

A Note on Data Privacy
Your health data is yours alone. Our interactive tools are "client-side," meaning the numbers you enter into our calculators never leave your computer and are never saved by our servers.
