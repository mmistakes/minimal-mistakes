### 🛡️ Request an Informational Correction

Maintaining absolute clinical accuracy across our library of over 330 research guides is my highest operational priority. If you are auditing a page on this platform and encounter any of the following discrepancies, please flag it for immediate review:

* **Temporal Staleness:** Outdated physiological data points or historical clinical definitions.
* **Integrity Breaks:** Broken hyperlinks or dead academic database portals.
* **Typographical Flaws:** Spelling mistakes, syntax errors, or formatting glitches.
* **Verification Gaps:** Missing primary journal citations or ambiguous source attributions.
* **Clarity Barriers:** Content blocks that feel needlessly complex, jargon-heavy, or potentially misleading to a senior reader.

To submit a formal correction request, please email the specific **page URL** along with a brief breakdown of the discrepancy. 

---

### ⚙️ How Correction Requests Are Audited

When a content flag or validation update is logged by a reader, I execute a structured data-repair protocol:

1. **Isolate & Verify:** The active page layout is checked immediately to analyze the reported error.
2. **Cross-Reference Secondary streams:** The underlying claim is re-verified against premier clinical engines, including the National Institutes of Health (NIH), the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), and the Mayo Clinic.
3. **Deploy the Code Fix:** The markdown file is updated natively to repair structural text or links.
4. **Upgrade the Citation Matrix:** New, robust peer-reviewed evidence layers are injected to maintain source integrity.
5. **Mark the Modification Lifecycle:** The live update is committed to production, and the absolute temporal accuracy is tracked via the `last_modified_at` data field at the top of the research brief.

This unyielding framework ensures that Aging Health remains an objective, transparent, and radically reliable resource for senior wellness advocacy.
